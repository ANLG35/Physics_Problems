# Section 0: Mathematical Foundations

## 7. Logic & Series

**Problem Statement:**
A bicycle is 10 meters from a wall and moves at $v_b = 1 \, \text{m/s}$. A fly flies at $v_f = 2 \, \text{m/s}$ between the bicycle and the wall until the bicycle reaches the wall. Find the total distance the fly travels.

---

### Solution

The key is to focus on **time** rather than the individual paths of the fly.

1. **Calculate the time until impact:**
   The bicycle needs to travel 10 meters at 1 m/s.
   $$t = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Speed}} = \frac{10 \, \text{m}}{1 \, \text{m/s}} = 10 \, \text{seconds}$$

2. **Calculate the fly's total travel distance:**
   Since the fly is moving at a constant speed of 2 m/s for the entire duration of 10 seconds:
   $$\text{Distance}_{fly} = \text{Speed}_{fly} \cdot t$$
   $$\text{Distance}_{fly} = 2 \, \text{m/s} \cdot 10 \, \text{s} = 20 \, \text{meters}$$

> **Answer:** The fly travels a total distance of **20 meters**.

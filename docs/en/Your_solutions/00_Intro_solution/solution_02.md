## 2. Systems of Equations

We need to find the values of $x$ and $y$ that satisfy the following system of linear equations:

1. $2x + 3y = 12$
2. $x - y = 1$

---

### Solution Steps

We can solve this system using the **substitution method**:

**Step 1: Isolate one variable.**
From the second equation ($x - y = 1$), we can easily isolate $x$:
$$x = y + 1$$

**Step 2: Substitute into the other equation.**
Plug $x = y + 1$ into the first equation ($2x + 3y = 12$):
$$2(y + 1) + 3y = 12$$

**Step 3: Solve for $y$.**
$$2y + 2 + 3y = 12$$
$$5y + 2 = 12$$
$$5y = 10$$
$$y = 2$$

**Step 4: Solve for $x$.**
Substitute $y = 2$ back into our expression for $x$:
$$x = 2 + 1$$
$$x = 3$$

> **Answer:** The solution to the system is $(x, y) = (3, 2)$.

---

### Geometric Interpretation

The point $(3, 2)$ is the unique point where the two lines $2x + 3y = 12$ and $x - y = 1$ intersect on the Cartesian plane.

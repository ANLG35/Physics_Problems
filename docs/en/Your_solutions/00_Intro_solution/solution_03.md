## 3. Proportionality

We use the Universal Law of Gravitation:
$$F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$

We want to find the new force $F'$ when:
* The distance is doubled: $r' = 2r$
* Both masses are halved: $m_1' = \frac{1}{2}m_1$ and $m_2' = \frac{1}{2}m_2$

---

### Solution Steps

The new force $F'$ is given by:
$$F' = G \frac{m_1' m_2'}{(r')^2}$$

Substitute the modified values into the equation:
$$F' = G \frac{(\frac{1}{2}m_1)(\frac{1}{2}m_2)}{(2r)^2}$$

Simplify the expression:
$$F' = G \frac{\frac{1}{4} m_1 m_2}{4r^2}$$
$$F' = \frac{1}{16} \left( G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} \right)$$

Since $F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$, we can substitute back:
$$F' = \frac{1}{16} F$$

> **Conclusion:** The force $F$ decreases by a factor of **16** (or is multiplied by $\frac{1}{16}$).

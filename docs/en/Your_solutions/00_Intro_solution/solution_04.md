## 4. Rearranging Formulas

The formula for the period of a simple pendulum is:
$$T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$$

We need to rearrange this equation to solve for $g$ (acceleration due to gravity).

---

### Algebraic Rearrangement Steps

1. **Divide both sides by $2\pi$:**
   $$\frac{T}{2\pi} = \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$$

2. **Square both sides to remove the square root:**
   $$\left(\frac{T}{2\pi}\right)^2 = \frac{L}{g}$$
   $$\frac{T^2}{4\pi^2} = \frac{L}{g}$$

3. **Cross-multiply to isolate $g$:**
   $$g \cdot T^2 = L \cdot 4\pi^2$$

4. **Solve for $g$:**
   $$g = \frac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}$$

> **Final Formula:** $g = \frac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}$

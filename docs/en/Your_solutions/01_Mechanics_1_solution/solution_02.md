## 2. Range Optimization
**Problem:** Show that maximum range $R(\theta) = \frac{v_0^2 \sin(2\theta)}{g}$ occurs at $\theta = 45^\circ$.

### Solution
1. **Differentiate:** Find the derivative of $R$ with respect to $\theta$:
   $$\frac{dR}{d\theta} = \frac{v_0^2}{g} \cdot \cos(2\theta) \cdot 2$$
2. **Maximize:** Set the derivative to zero:
   $$\frac{2v_0^2}{g} \cos(2\theta) = 0$$
3. **Solve for $\theta$:**
   $$\cos(2\theta) = 0 \implies 2\theta = 90^\circ \implies \theta = 45^\circ$$

> **Result:** The range is maximized at a launch angle of **$45^\circ$**.

## Problem: Centripetal Acceleration at the Equator

**Given:**
* Earth's radius ($R$): $6378 \text{ km} = 6.378 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$
* Earth's rotation period ($T$): $24 \text{ hours} = 86400 \text{ s}$

---

### 1. Angular Velocity ($\omega$)
The Earth completes one full rotation ($2\pi$ radians) in 24 hours:
$$\omega = \frac{2\pi}{T} = \frac{2\pi}{86400} \approx 7.27 \times 10^{-5} \text{ rad/s}$$

---

### 2. Centripetal Acceleration ($a_c$)
The formula for centripetal acceleration is:
$$a_c = \omega^2 R$$

Plugging in the values:
$$a_c = (7.27 \times 10^{-5})^2 \times (6.378 \times 10^6)$$
$$a_c \approx 0.0337 \text{ m/s}^2$$

**Result:** The centripetal acceleration of a person standing on the equator is approximately **$0.0337 \text{ m/s}^2$**.

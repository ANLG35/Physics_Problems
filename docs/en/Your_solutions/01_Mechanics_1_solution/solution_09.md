## Problem: Momentum Comparison

**Definition:** Momentum ($p$) is the product of an object's mass ($m$) and its velocity ($v$):
$$p = m \times v$$

---

### 1. Momentum of the Fly
* Mass ($m_f$): $2\text{ g} = 0.002\text{ kg}$
* Velocity ($v_f$): $10\text{ m/s}$

$$p_{fly} = 0.002 \times 10 = 0.02\text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$$

---

### 2. Momentum of the Tennis Ball
* Mass ($m_b$): $60\text{ g} = 0.06\text{ kg}$
* Velocity ($v_b$): $1\text{ m/s}$

$$p_{ball} = 0.06 \times 1 = 0.06\text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$$

---

### Comparison Result
Since $0.06 > 0.02$, the **tennis ball** has a greater momentum.

**Result:** The tennis ball has **3 times** more momentum than the fly.
